# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the central documentation hub for OctoAcme's project management processes. This collection of guides provides a comprehensive framework for delivering customer-focused projects using structured workflows, clear ownership, and iterative delivery principles.

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a customer-first, data-informed approach to project management that emphasizes iterative delivery and psychological safety. Our methodology is built on clear ownership principles where each project has a named Project Manager coordinating delivery and a Product Manager defining outcomes. The framework supports cross-functional teams—including developers, QA/testing, and stakeholders—working collaboratively to deliver small, testable increments that maximize customer value. Core artifacts such as project charters, roadmaps, sprint backlogs, acceptance criteria, risk registers, and retrospective notes ensure transparency and alignment throughout the project lifecycle.

The OctoAcme project lifecycle encompasses five key phases: **Initiation** validates business needs and aligns stakeholders through a project one-pager; **Planning** establishes scope, resources, milestones, and dependencies; **Execution** involves building, testing, and iterating with regular standups and progress tracking; **Release** coordinates deployment activities with verification and announcements; and **Close & Retrospective** captures learnings and improvement actions. This structured approach is complemented by a consistent communication cadence including weekly PM-Product syncs, twice-weekly team standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations as needed.

Risk management and quality assurance are woven throughout the process. Teams maintain risk registers that track identification, assessment, mitigation, and monitoring of potential issues, with clear escalation paths from team level through PM and Product Lead to executive sponsors. Communication strategies emphasize a single source of truth for project status, structured templates for weekly updates and incident communications, and stakeholder-specific engagement plans. The roles and personas framework clearly defines responsibilities for developers (building reliable, maintainable code), Product Managers (defining customer value and prioritizing backlogs), and Project Managers (coordinating delivery and managing risks), ensuring that all team members understand their accountabilities and collaboration touchpoints.

OctoAcme's commitment to continuous improvement is reflected in our retrospective practices and emphasis on learning from both successes and challenges. By measuring impact through defined success metrics and iterating based on evidence, teams continuously refine their approaches while maintaining high standards for test coverage, observability, and documentation. This comprehensive framework enables teams to deliver projects on time, within scope, and with consistent quality that meets both customer and business objectives.
